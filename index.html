The Thesis

The data center is being rebuilt from the inside out.

AI workloads have made the assumptions behind the old infrastructure model obsolete. Rack power density has climbed from roughly 10 kW in conventional enterprise deployments to well over 300 kW in leading AI clusters — with projections of 1 MW per rack by 2029. The electrical, thermal, and software architecture built over the past three decades was not designed for this and cannot be patched to handle it.

Five graduate researchers each investigated a distinct domain of AI data-center infrastructure over 11.5 weeks. The streams converge on one conclusion: the constraint is no longer capital — it is knowing where to deploy it.

My Role

My Contribution: Silicon & Accelerators

I authored the silicon research stream, analyzing where value accrues as AI hardware scales. My core finding: transistor count is no longer the binding constraint — power delivery, interconnect bandwidth, and thermal density are. I mapped the accelerator landscape (hyperscaler custom silicon versus merchant GPUs), sized the addressable market, and developed an investment thesis centered on the compute-optimization and optical-interconnect layer — the ‘picks and shovels’ of the AI decade — before presenting recommendations to the investment committee.
